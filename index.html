🎯 The Core Mission

Every single cell in our body shares the exact same DNA sequence[cite: 2]. Yet, a brain cell, a muscle cell, and a pancreatic cell behave entirely differently because they switch on different subsets of genes[cite: 2].

Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) lets us capture these distinct transcript patterns one cell at a time. However, standard software pipelines often use a "straight-line ruler" to group cells[cite: 1, 2]. This flattens and hides critical, rare cell types in the noise.

Our Goal: We developed the **Information-Geometric Multiscale Clustering (IGMC)** framework to replace outdated, rigid clustering methods with an adaptive, noise-aware model capable of identifying rare cells and tracking complex diabetic mutations.

🩺 Clinical Focus

We targeted HNF1A-driven monogenic diabetes (MODY).

Rather than deleting cells outright, this single transcription-factor mutation pushes healthy pancreatic beta cells into abnormal, transitioning intermediate states[cite: 1]. Tracing this transitional process is the ultimate stress test for our shape-validation and rare-population metrics[cite: 1]!

⏳ Foundational History

Macosko et al. (2015)

Previously, researchers had to sequence bulk chunks of tissue, which only provided an "average" reading of millions of blended cells[cite: 2]. Macosko et al. revolutionized biology by developing Drop-seq, isolating thousands of individual cells in microscopic oil droplets to map their individual gene expression profiles[cite: 2].

⚙️ The Multi-Platform Problem

FIRM (Ming et al., 2022)

When combining biological data from different laboratories, different sequencing platforms introduce systemic technical noise (batch effects)[cite: 1, 2]. Data integration frameworks like FIRM help remove platform variations to align cells based on real, shared biology[cite: 1, 2].

📊 Evaluated Datasets

  • 10X PBMC 3k: Standard dataset of 2,638 immune cells used for fast iteration and pipeline verification[cite: 1].
  • Islet Reference (Baron, Muraro, Segerstolpe): Used as our healthy pancreas baseline with known cell types[cite: 1].
  • HNF1A Mutant Pancreas: Used to evaluate and locate cells transitioning toward disease states[cite: 1].

🛠️ Meet the 4 Core Pillars of our Algorithm

Pillar 1

The Smart Ruler 📏

Instead of treating all genes with standard linear distance, our mathematical metric accounts for Poisson-like count noise[cite: 1, 2]. It automatically scales down distance for highly variable, noisy genes, while magnifying subtle differences in quiet, low-count transcripts[cite: 1].

Pillar 2

The Ink-Drop Test 💧

Traditional clustering requires hand-tuning an arbitrary "resolution knob" to choose cluster counts[cite: 1]. We run continuous random walks (diffusion simulations) across our network; real biological populations naturally trap the walk and show up as stable plateaus over time[cite: 1, 2].

Pillar 3

Islands vs. Highways 🛣️

We apply topological shape tests to see if a cell population forms isolated groupings (islands indicating separate types) or a continuous progression (highways indicating transitioning cells)[cite: 1].

Pillar 4

Calibrated Forecasts 🌤️

Instead of forcing an overconfident, single label on every cell, our split-conformal statistical boundaries identify transitioning cells and assign them range-bound labels with 90% confidence guarantees[cite: 1].
