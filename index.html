📜 Topic Review & History

In the past, scientists had to analyze tissue samples in bulk, which averaged out the unique characteristics of different cells[cite: 2]. This changed with the breakthrough work by Macosko et al. (2015)[cite: 1, 2].

By inventing a microfluidic technology (Drop-seq) to isolate thousands of individual cells in nanoliter-sized droplets, they could measure the exact mRNA fingerprint of individual cells[cite: 2]. This launched the modern era of single-cell biology, allowing us to build complete cellular maps of our tissues[cite: 2].

⚙️ The Integration Problem (FIRM)

Because datasets are collected by different labs using different technologies (like SMART-Seq2 vs. 10X), technical differences can introduce noise and cause cells to cluster by platform instead of biological type[cite: 1, 2].

Methods like FIRM (Ming et al., 2022) correct these platform variations to ensure cells align based on true biology, preserving subtle biological details without overcorrecting the data[cite: 1, 2].

🎯 Our Aim & Clinical Importance

In conditions like HNF1A-driven monogenic diabetes, genetic mutations do not simply wipe out cell types; they slowly push cells into unstable, transitioning intermediate states[cite: 1].

Standard distance measures miss these transitions because they force cells into neat, simplified clusters[cite: 1, 2]. By replacing traditional rulers with a noise-aware algorithm, we can trace exactly how these cells drift on their path toward disease[cite: 1].

❌ Standard PCA + Leiden

  • Uses straight-line Euclidean distance, ignoring natural transcript variance[cite: 1, 2].
  • Requires manual tuning of an arbitrary "resolution knob" to find clusters[cite: 1].
  • Fails to resolve rare populations, absorbing them into larger groups[cite: 1].
  • Forces labels on cells, failing to flag ambiguous transitioning phenotypes[cite: 1].

✅ Our IGMC Framework

  • Smart Ruler: Scales mathematical distances to prioritize low-expression signals[cite: 1].
  • Ink-Drop Walks: Finds stable biological communities naturally[cite: 1].
  • Islands vs. Highways: Math-based validation of discrete types vs. continuums[cite: 1].
  • Conformal Sets: Clear and honest confidence boundaries on every single cell label[cite: 1].

🛠️ Inside the Algorithm

1. Information-Geometric Metric

Uses Negative Binomial modeling properties to ensure that quiet, low-count transcripts are evaluated with higher sensitivity than noisy high-count ones[cite: 1].

2. Markov Stability Community Search

Drips mathematical "ink" on the cell network and monitors where fluid is held across various walk times to identify natural cell type groups[cite: 1].

3. Topological Shape Test

Analyzes the spatial patterns of cell communities to determine if they form isolated islands (distinct types) or pathways (transition lines)[cite: 1].

4. Split-Conformal Prediction

Replaces simple, single-label guesses with robust confidence boundaries, letting the model report when a cell is transitioning or highly ambiguous[cite: 1].
