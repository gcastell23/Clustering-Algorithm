# NOVELTY THESIS
The negative-binomial VAE already carries a canonical statistical-manifold geometry — the Fisher-Rao pullback metric M(z)=J_f(z)^T I_NB(f(z)) J_f(z) that its own decoder Jacobian and count likelihood induce on the latent space — and our contribution is to make that single geometric object do all four inferential jobs at once: define the inter-cell distance, be scanned across scales by Markov stability, be probed for discrete-versus-continuous structure by persistent homology, and supply a geometry-native nonconformity score whose conformal prediction sets carry finite-sample, scale-nested coverage — converting scRNA-seq clustering from a stack of mutually inconsistent Euclidean heuristics into one coherent, uncertainty-calibrated inference on the manifold of the count model.

# POSITIONING
The delta is coherence-from-one-geometry plus two genuinely new algorithmic pieces, not any single component. vs scVI: scVI supplies the NB-VAE latent but then throws away the geometry its generative model induces, running kNN/Leiden under plain L2 on the posterior mean; we replace L2 with the Fisher-Rao pullback M(z)=J^T I_NB(f) J — the metric scVI implicitly defines but never uses. vs GAIA (Cai 2025, our most dangerous competitor): GAIA uses the closed-form multinomial Fisher-Rao (sqrt-embedding, d=2·arccos Σ√(p_i q_i)) directly in gene space, treating genes as independent, static, single-scale, no topology, no confidence; we use the *learned, correlation-aware, denoised* pullback on a generative latent and add multiscale + topology + conformal. We therefore cannot and must not claim to be first to bring information geometry to scRNA-seq — only first to (i) pull the Fisher-Rao metric back through an NB generative decoder at scale and (ii) unify it with multiscale community detection, a topological discrete/continuous test, and calibrated coverage. vs Arvanitidis 2022 (Pulling Back Information Geometry): the pullback M=J_f^T I(f) J_f framework already exists and explicitly names NB/Poisson/categorical decoders; our novelty is the first single-cell instantiation, the scalability machinery, and everything built on top — the metric itself is an instantiation, not a new framework, and reviewers will know this. vs single-resolution Leiden: we replace the hand-tuned resolution with Markov-stability scanning (full exp(-tL), resolution-limit-free CPM) and VI-plateau scale selection, reporting a hierarchy. vs standard Markov stability / PyGenStability: vanilla MS uses a Euclidean autocovariance on a Euclidean-built graph; we build the graph on the pullback metric and (stretch novelty) replace the autocovariance inner product with an information divergence between walker distributions and the stationary law on the simplex. vs scTDA / Rizvi 2017: scTDA uses 1-Pearson + Mapper with permutation p-values on *genes*; we use the pullback metric as the filtration base and turn persistent H0/H1 into a *per-region discreteness statistic that steers the clustering*, with conformal calibration on *cell labels*. vs López-De-Castro 2025 / scConform / Ding 2023: they wrap conformal around a fixed supervised softmax classifier; we define a geometry-native score (Fisher-Rao distance-to-centroid or NB neg-log-likelihood) and make our multiscale clusters themselves the Mondrian groups, giving nested per-scale conditional coverage plus a covariate-shift-weighted variant for case/control. vs PHATE/Multiscale PHATE/DPT: they use an information-geometric potential distance for *visualization* with heuristic scale selection and no calibrated confidence and no count-model Fisher metric.

# CONTRIBUTIONS
- NB Fisher-Rao pullback metric for single cells, made computable at scale: instantiate M(z)=J_f(z)^T I_NB(f(z)) J_f(z) with the closed-form NB Fisher information (per-gene term 1/(mu+mu^2/theta)) and the scVI decoder Jacobian by reverse-mode autodiff; defeat the decoder non-identifiability of the induced geometry with a decoder-ensemble/Bayesian expected metric bar-M(z)=(1/K)Σ_k J_{g_k}^T I J_{g_k}; and make geodesic distances tractable for 10^5-10^6 cells via a kNN metric-graph with local-Riemannian edge lengths and landmark/Nystrom shortest-paths.
- Information-geometric Markov stability: run multiscale community detection (full continuous-time stability r(t,H)=trace[H^T(Pi e^{-tL_rw}-pi pi^T)H], resolution-limit-free CPM optimizer, PyGenStability backbone) on the pullback-metric graph, and — as the load-bearing algorithmic novelty — replace the Euclidean autocovariance with a Fisher-Rao/alpha-divergence between each node's walker distribution P(t)_i and the stationary pi on the probability simplex, with scales selected by Variation-of-Information plateaus rather than one hand-set resolution.
- A formal, calibrated discrete-versus-continuous test coupled to scale: build a DTM/Vietoris-Rips filtration on the pullback distance, read discreteness off persistent H0 and continuity/branch-loops off persistent H1, attach Fasy-style bootstrap significance to separate topological signal from noise, and use the resulting per-region statistic to decide locally whether to partition (discrete type) or preserve a continuum (e.g., the HNF1A dedifferentiation gradient) rather than forcing a global resolution.
- Geometry-native, scale-nested conformal confidence: split/Mondrian conformal with a nonconformity score taken directly from the manifold (Fisher-Rao distance-to-cluster-centroid or negative NB log-likelihood under the cluster's fitted model) rather than a generic softmax; the multiscale clusters serve as the Mondrian partition, yielding a nested sequence of conditional-coverage guarantees across scales; plus a covariate-shift-weighted (Tibshirani) variant for healthy->MODY calibration and an autoencoder-reconstruction conformal OOD flag for HNF1A-perturbed cells absent from a healthy reference.
- Internal-consistency / unification result: demonstrate that distance, the stability functional, the filtration, and the nonconformity score are all derived from the *same* Fisher-Rao metric, so the pipeline is self-consistent (VI selection is information-theoretic, PH stability is bounded in the same metric, conformal scores are geodesic) — the argument that this is one inference, not four bolted-together tools.
- The HNF1A-MODY islet benchmark as a new joint evaluation task that simultaneously scores rare-cell-type recovery (epsilon/delta/stellate and HNF1A-perturbed beta substates), continuous-state-axis fidelity (maturation, ER-stress, HNF1A-activity via FXYD2/SLC2A2/PDX1/TMEM27), and calibrated per-cell coverage — a rare-subpopulation + confidence stress test no existing single-cell benchmark provides.

# RECOMMENDED EXPERIMENTS
- Figure 1 (schematic + thesis): one Fisher-Rao pullback object feeding four roles (distance, Markov-stability functional, persistent-homology filtration, conformal score), making the 'one geometry, not four tools' claim visually explicit.
- Metric-ablation on simulated ground truth (splatter/dyngen/dyntoy with planted discrete+continuous mixtures and rare populations at 0.1-5% abundance): rare-population recall/macro-F1 as a function of abundance for our Fisher-Rao pullback vs (a) Euclidean pullback M=J^T J, (b) closed-form simplex FR (GAIA), (c) plain L2 on scVI latent, (d) log1p+PCA+Leiden, (e) PHATE, (f) Markov stability on a Euclidean graph — the causal test that the NB Fisher geometry, specifically, is what helps.
- Identifiability experiment: decoder ensemble size K=1,5,10,20; report variance of the metric tensor and stability (ARI/VI) of geodesic distances and cluster assignments with vs without marginalization — the direct answer to the Hauberg/Syrota objection.
- Calibration audit with leave-one-donor-out on GSE84133 (Baron) and E-MTAB-5061 (Segerstolpe): empirical vs nominal coverage, mean/median set size, size-stratified coverage, and per-cell-type coverage; compare split vs Mondrian vs clustered conformal (Ding) and the geometry-native score vs THR/APS/RAPS; explicitly plot per-rare-type coverage for epsilon/delta/stellate.
- Covariate-shift stress test: healthy->T2D and healthy->HNF1A-perturbed calibration; show marginal coverage degrades under naive conformal and is restored by weighted/covariate-shift conformal; report conformal OOD detection of perturbed cells at controlled FPR.
- Discrete-vs-continuous validation: on data known discrete (PBMC immune types) vs known continuous (a differentiation trajectory), show the persistent H0/H1 statistic separates the two regimes with Fasy significance; then apply to the beta-cell compartment to adjudicate HNF1A discrete-switch vs continuous-dedifferentiation.
- Full scIB benchmark (Luecken 2022): 14-metric battery with isolated-label F1/ASW, cLISI, graph connectivity, overall=0.4*batch+0.6*bio, against the scIB leaderboard (Harmony, scVI, scANVI, Scanorama); must be competitive on bio-conservation while uniquely adding calibrated coverage and average set size stratified by cell-type frequency.
- Scalability curve: wall-clock and memory vs N (1k->250k cells) with the landmark/graph-geodesic and sparse-filtration approximations, plus a certified approximation-error report; demonstrate a run on the 2025 48-donor / 245,878-cell atlas.
- Component ablation table: marginal contribution of each module (pullback metric, information-geometric MS, PH discrete/continuous test, conformal layer) to rare-population F1 and to coverage — proving no module is dead weight.
- Biological result with orthogonal validation: a conformally-certified aberrant/intermediate beta-cell state along the HNF1A axis, characterized by FXYD2/SLC2A2/PDX1/TMEM27 loss and ER-stress up (DDIT3/HSPA5/ATF4), validated by AUCell module scores and, ideally, smFISH or an independent MODY/perturbation cohort — the discovery needed to lift the paper above a pure methods contribution.
- Robustness to decoder misspecification: NB vs ZINB vs Poisson emission, showing the geometry/coverage are stable when the likelihood is correctly specified (per Svensson) and degrade gracefully otherwise.
